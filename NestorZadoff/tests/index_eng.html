NÉSTOR ZADOFF


Born in Buenos Aires, Argentina, in 1952.


National Music Professor graduated from the National Conservatory in 1973 and Musical Arts graduate (UNA).

He studied Harmony with Erwin Leuchter and Choral Conduction with Antonio Russo at the Juan José Castro Conservatory.

He obtained a scholarship from the French government, to specialize himself in Renaissance Choral Music between 1976 and 1979.

He studied with Jean-Michel Vaccaro and under his guidance he obtained the degree of Doctor in Musicology at the Francois Rabelais University of Tours in 1979.

He returned that year to Argentina and reinserted himself in the musical environment of Buenos Aires, conducting choirs and chamber ensembles.


He has been for 30 years conductor of the Coro Nacional de Jóvenes (24 Prizes in different international choir contests,

GP of Florilège Vocal de Tours, 1st Prizes at Porto Alegre, Spittal, Lindenholzhausen) and currently conductor of Grupo Coral Divertimento

(GCD, symphonic, www.gcdivertimento.com.ar).


He is Senior Lecturer for Choir Conduction, Choral Stylistics and Choral Arrangements Compositions at the UNA

(Arts National University, Music Department DAMUS, www.artesmusicales.org).

He conducts courses and choral workshops on a regular basis and is also frequently invited

to conduct choirs in Argentina and abroad, specially in France, Spain, Brazil and Chile.


He is author of more than 100 choral arrangements, 11 of which have been awarded in diverse contests:

Nacional 1985, Municipal 1987, Eistedfood 1993, Ars Nova de Rosario 2000, Fondo Nacional de las Artes 2007 and AAMCANT 2011.

His musicological research and arrangements have been published by GCC Editions in Argentina,

Editions A Coeur Joie in France and Kjos in USA.

He is in charge of the Ancient Music Department in GCC Editions.


He was named Honorary Membership to CAMU, Consejo Argentino de la Música (Music Council of Argentina),

in 1998 and in 1999 he won the "New Talents of the 1989-1998 Decade" Konex Award in Choir Conduction.


He was given the Choir Conductor of the 1999-2008 decade Konex Award in 2009.











FREE TRANSCRIPTIONS OF CHORAL WORKS FROM THE XVI CENTURY



Over the last several years, I have returned to my work as a researcher specializing in Early Music.

In times when the music publishing industry is going through a deep crisis of global dimensions,

I decided to offer my work free of charge through this platform to whoever would be interested

in rare vocal repertoire of the Renaissance period, which, I expect, is not currently available in print.

You will find transcriptions to modern notation and with updated musicological criteria of

French chansons, Italian villanesche, and Spanish madrigals written during the 16th century

and beginning of the 17th century. I hope these humble contributions will allow a renaissance

of these works through chamber groups and choirs of any nationality.



(Musicological) PRINCIPLES USED IN TRANSCRIPTIONS



• Rhythmic values were halved.

• Editorial accidentals are suggested only for the specific pitches in question.

• Passages in which the text is confusing in the original score will be expressed

  between [ ] indicating that they are editorial suggestions.

  This happens specially in earlier works as from Josquin and Nola.

  In later works (Cornet, Lassus) the composers and editors were very precise.

• Bar-lines were not used in that historical period. To facilitate reading,

  my proposal includes individual bar-lines in each voice part

  which are placed according to each line’s phrasing,

  always considering the natural accentuation of the words.

• In homophonic passages, bar-lines will most likely coincide in all parts,

  but in imitative sections (very common in works of Franco-Flemish composers)

  each voice part will have its specific phrasing.
